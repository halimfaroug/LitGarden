Module 5: Samples of Poetry (Winter by William Shakespeare)

Learn

Winter Song: A Close Reading. Analyzing William Shakespeare's Love's Labour's Lost. William Shakespeare (1564–1616), widely considered the finest writer in the English language, was a master of capturing the human experience through plays, sonnets, and poetry. This presentation explores 'Winter Song,' a poem from Act V, Scene 2 of his comedy Love's Labour's Lost. While written over four hundred years ago, the imagery remains stark and familiar. It depicts winter in 16th-century England—a time of crisp new-fallen snow, red noses, and the sharp contrast between the freezing outdoors and the warmth of the kitchen fire.
